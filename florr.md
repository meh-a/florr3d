# Visuals

All visuals in florr.io lerp when moving, and have thick borders.

The font is Ubuntu bold.

The player visually is a yellow circle. The petals from the player's loadout orbit the player, dealing damage to the mobs they hit.

The loadout slots are a light gray color, with thick borders.


# Loadout
The Loadout is where all petals that are in use are displayed. Every flower has a primary loadout and a secondary loadout: only petals in your primary loadout will appear around or on your flower. You can switch your entire primary loadout with your secondary loadout by pressing R, or switch individual petals with its slot's respective numeric hotkey or clicking the petal. Holding the petal allows you to rearrange it or return it to your inventory.

The player's loadout has 5 slots.

Holding L will display all your saved loadouts in the secondary loadout, with the first petal in the loadout serving as the icon. Blank loadouts simply show a number. Pressing O will instantly save your loadout: pressing L+Number will load the specific primary loadout saved to that number, and K+Number will load the secondary loadout. A maximum of ten builds can be stored.

You can also change your rotation speed manually: you can use Q to reduce your rotation speed and E to increase it. The slowest your rotation speed can be is 30% of your maximum rotation speed, which depends on your Petal Rotation talent.

To save loadouts on mobile, hold X and you can switch loadouts by tapping the secondary petal slots. Each loadout uses the icon of the first petal in the loadout as its icon.

# Petals

Petals are the main tools and weapons used by all players in florr.io. All players start out with five petal slots and can gain up to five additional slots (adding up to ten) with the Loadout talent. Players also have an equal number of secondary petal slots. A petal in a secondary slot can be switched with the petal in the primary slot directly above. There are currently 118 petals; however, M28, the developer, is going to add more in the future.

When colliding with mobs, flowers and other petals, they deal damage. Petals also receive damage from collisions, which lowers their health. When a petal's health is depleted, it will be destroyed temporarily and reappear after a certain amount of time (the petal's cooldown time).

# Mobs

Mobs are the main antagonists of florr.io. They are also the main form of progressing through the game by dropping Petals. Each mob has a unique appearance and Petal drops. Some mobs also have special abilities, such as firing projectiles towards you. Mobs will also give you XP, which may level you up which will then give you Talent Points for game progression. All mobs also have an Armor stat (reduces damage taken by the value of the Armor). Mobs spawn in one Area and might have a specific small area where they are more frequently spawned. Some mobs may get special attacks at the Super and Unique rarity, e.g. Hornet. **Rock**: A stationary mob. (drops rock)
**Ladybug**: Moves around slowly. Rare+ Ladybugs will move towards the player when attacked. (drops rose and light)
**Bee**: Moves around slowly in a sine wave pattern. Rare+ Bees will swerve towards the player at high speeds in a sine wave pattern when attacked. (drops stinger)

The rock petal is a rock with high health but low reload.
The rose petal is a healing petal that gets absorbed by the player when they're health is low.
The light petal spawns multiple petals (~3), all with low health and damage.
The stinger petal has high reload and low health, but very high damage.
